Treatments

Dental veneers or contact lenses

They are generally recommended for aesthetic reasons and involve only the front face of the teeth, are increasingly thinner and require little wear. They are used in cases of stained or restored teeth, when the patient wants to improve aesthetics.

Aesthetic restorations

Improve or restore the aesthetics of the smile from treatments such as tooth whitening, contact lenses, veneers and ceramic crowns. Nowadays dentistry lives its best moment in aesthetics, with materials and techniques that bring naturalness.

Tooth whitening

Anyone can have their natural teeth whitened. In some cases, when the patient has many restorations and / or prostheses, it may be necessary to combine other treatments for the aesthetic result to be satisfactory.

Halitosis (bad breath)

It is a disease that affects more than 40% of the population, but most people do not feel their halitosis. Almost 90% are of oral origin, but it can also be a sign of some disorder or systemic disease. There is YES treatment and we can help!

Dentures

They are used when there is total absence of teeth, in this case we always indicate the placement of implants for the manufacture of prostheses of the type Protocol on Implants (see the video above).

Prostheses on Implants

The prostheses fixed on the implants have as a major advantage the fact that they do not come loose during chewing, providing greater comfort, safety and efficiency in addition to greater retention and aesthetics in relation to mobile prostheses.

Fixed prothesis

It is the most used method to restore comfort and aesthetics to patients who need to replace one or more teeth. Currently, most of them are made of pure porcelain.

Osseointegrated Implants

They are made of Titanium and placed through surgery to replace missing teeth. With the convenience that our clinic offers, the patient may choose to use local anesthetic or intravenous sedation applied by the anesthesiologist.

Periodontics

Periodontal disease is the involvement of the tissues that surround the teeth and that leads to inflammation of the gingival tissue and / or resorption of the bone that is around the roots of the teeth. Regular consultation with the periodontist is essential for oral health.
